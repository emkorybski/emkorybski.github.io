Why clean with ozone?

Original content inspired by facts and findings about ozone, from SmellFixers

Many things are being said about ozone, especially in the pandemic times - from extreme praise to extreme criticism.
Both sides of the argument highlight subjectively what is suitable for their purpose.
Our objective in this article is to collect and present the pros and cons of using ozone, when it makes sense and when it does not - to allow people to decide for themselves.
Let's start with the basics.

Ozone is the unstable particle made of 3 oxygen atoms as opposed to 2 atoms we would see in a regular stable oxygen particle that we breathe in the air.
This makes ozone very reactive, making it very strong oxidant. And this is where most danger of ozone would come from - its high reactivity leading to peroxidation and effectively destroying lipids in cells. Ozone would act the same way on bacterial viral or fungal cells ason human body cells. Ozone in high concentration is toxic because of the way it reacts not only with organic matter but also artificial chemicals that make up our environment.

Chlorine has similar properties as ozone, only much weaker. It lasts much longer in the environment than ozone and these longer exposures (over years of using chlorine-based disinfectant or using a chlorine-sanitized swimming pools) can have much worse poisoning effect than ozone, that takes very short time to break down even if it is not oxidizing.

Now, to the nitty gritty of indoor space sanitation with ozone.
Ozone, when released from ozone-generating machine has a transparent gas form that disperses if given free space, and concentrates when the spece is limited, room sealed off.
For the original toxic concentration to break down significantly, to level that is only irritating for your breathing apparatus but not toxic it takes on average at least 30 minutes is needed (based on the concntration of ozone produced by 7 grams/hour machine in a room of approx. 50 cubic meters).
It is important to realize that ozone is super reactive and it will also react with chemicals from paints, preservants air fresheners and other non-organic substances typically present in our home environment, creating sometimes chemicals that could be dangerous on longer exposure. It is hard to determine as we have a lot artifcial chemicals around us at home (air fresheners is one of most obvious examples) whether indeed such harmful substance will be created during ozoning so it is considered best practice to start air filtration even during the ozone production process, ideally using purifiers with HEPA filters. This practice - that we adhere to in our sanitation service - ensures that both destroyed organiz matter and neutralized chemicals

On a general note, our sense of smell in most cases will best tell us when we should not be around ozone as concentration is getting dangerous.

It is totally unline this with ozone. it is similar that way to chlorine. All you will need to do after ozoning is just hoover the floors and wipe surfaces exposed to ozone, to remove a film of neutralized/dead germs spores and particles.

Toxicity and how do you prevent ozone poisoning?

It is not difficult to handle ozone properly and in most situations when using concentrated ozone as disinfectant in your household, you don't need a measurement tool or counter to stay safe and get the most from your ozone treatment.
Many of us already have anti-smog or surgical masks that are being used during the still ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.
These masks can be a good protection if you are entering a space where there is ozone in toxic concentration - you simply will not have to jump in quickly but omfortably adjust your air purifier or ozone generator program settings.

If the exposure to concentrated ozone is very short, i.e. less than 30 seconds then such mask or covering your nose and mouth tightly should suffice.
If you have to stay in a space where ozone is in high and toxic concentration than using a mask with active coal filter is a must.
Remember that even small concentrations of ozone that are higher than normal may cause irritation (so strictly speaking cause no damage but cause shortness of breathing and make you cough).
Ozone has a very specific smell and is easy to detect - when concentration is getting larger your breating apparatus and will certainly tell you that.
